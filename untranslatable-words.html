Beautiful Japanese Words With No English Equivalent

木漏れ日 (komorebi)
"sunlight filtering through leaves"
The dappled, shifting light you see scattered across a forest floor.
物の哀れ (mono no aware)
"the pathos of things"
A gentle sadness that comes from knowing beautiful things don't last.
侘び寂び (wabi-sabi)
"beauty in imperfection"
Finding worth in things that are worn, incomplete, or impermanent.
積読 (tsundoku)
"buying books and letting them pile up unread"
A word every book lover secretly needs.
森林浴 (shinrin-yoku)
"forest bathing"
Spending time in nature simply to relax, not to hike or exercise.
一期一会 (ichigo ichie)
"one time, one meeting"
Treasuring an encounter because it will never happen in exactly the same way again.
縁 (en)
"an invisible bond"
The sense of fate or connection that brings two people into each other's lives.
甘え (amae)
"sweet dependence"
The comfort of leaning on someone who you know will take care of you.
幽玄 (yuugen)
"a profound, mysterious beauty"
A feeling too deep and subtle to fully put into words.
生き甲斐 (ikigai)
"a reason for being"
The thing that makes getting up in the morning worth it.

Cool Japanese Phrases & Mottos

不撓不屈 (futou fukutsu)
"unbending, unyielding"
Refusing to break no matter how many setbacks come.
初心忘るべからず (shoshin wasuru bekarazu)
"never forget your beginner's mind"
Stay humble and eager to learn, even once you're an expert.
七転び八起き (nana korobi ya oki)
"fall seven times, get up eight"
A saying about resilience through repeated failure.
好きこそものの上手なれ (suki koso mono no jouzu nare)
"what you love, you naturally get good at"
Passion is the best teacher.
石の上にも三年 (ishi no ue ni mo sannen)
"three years sitting on a stone"
Patience and persistence eventually pay off.
塵も積もれば山となる (chiri mo tsumoreba yama to naru)
"even dust piles into a mountain"
Small, consistent effort adds up to something big.
